Open Source Software vs. Proprietary Software

Which of Ramsey's observations about FOSS and proprietary software have you observed in your own home or workplace (does not have to be GIS related)? Or, if you don't think Ramsey's observations are legitimate, justify why.

In his presentation, Paul Ramsey has emphasized five managerial reasons to consider using an Open Source Software. These are –

  1. Cloud readiness
  2. Lack of license liability
  3. Flexibility
  4. Staff development and recruitment
  5. Market power

I have observed several scenarios at my workplace and academic career where Ramsey’s arguments hold good.

Definitely, Startups recognize the value of having free and open source software (FOSS) options available which minimizes their initial investment of purchasing a software license to a huge extent. And having the capability to customize or modify the software as per the requirement proves to be very helpful. That said, I have worked at a well-established, one of the largest multinational IT company, even they used to use FOSS such as OpenOffice, Zimbra, etc. back in 2013.

For personal project works, purchasing a proprietary software license is very costly as well as it holds a legal liability. I strongly support FOSS because in such cases software like QGIS, Python, etc. provides us almost all functionalities that a proprietary software would provide; and also, one does not have to worry about publication or distribution of his/her work.

Although the other reasons namely flexibility, staff development, cloud readiness, are true, they are not strong arguments to switch from proprietary to FOSS. At my previous workplace, the organization had a sophisticated, customized extension built on top of ArcGIS for pipeline integrity management; and our team was working on developing a custom web application for health safety environment (HSE) monitoring using ESRI products. Thus, I think that proprietary software also provides flexibility, staff development and cloud readiness. In addition to that, the vendors also provide full technical support.

To me, all these scenarios point to one major benefit of having FOSS which is cost-effectiveness. However, that too can have one drawback; using a FOSS may not be cost-effective in terms of time. As a developer one might require to invest time in developing a software as per the project requirement; while as a user, one might require to put lot of efforts and time in learning the software on their own.

After studying this lesson, would you advocate the use of a pure FOSS approach for your GIS work, or are there certain elements of proprietary software that you would retain as part of a hybrid approach?

After reading the reference articles provided in this assignment + the example given by Paul Ramsey on hybrid approach adopted by Pierce County, Washington + my academic coursework where I have used ArcGIS, QGIS, PostgreSQL, Python and various libraries in R, I would advocate a hybrid approach because having a proprietary software makes life easy and FOSS software provides freedom to use, modify and redistribute.
